4  IRR Deep Dive

4.1 What IRR Actually Means

IRR is the discount rate at which the present value of all future cashflows equals the initial investment. In lending terms:

IRR is the rate at which: PV(all EMIs received) = Amount disbursed

There is no closed-form solution — IRR is always solved numerically (which is why Excel and bank systems exist).

4.1.1 IRR vs Interest Rate

These are not the same thing. Here’s why:

Dimension Interest Rate IRR
What it measures Stated cost of borrowing True annualized return on capital
Accounts for fees? No Yes
Accounts for timing? Partially Fully
Accounts for amortization? No Yes
Comparable across products? Poorly Yes

A home loan at 9% interest rate may have an IRR of ~9.5–10% once processing fees are included. A checkout EMI with 0% interest but 6% subvention may have a 20%+ IRR.

4.2 The Prepayment Paradox

Here’s something that seems contradictory at first:

Prepayment increases IRR but banks still hate it.

4.2.1 Why IRR Goes Up with Prepayment

If a customer prepays a loan:

  • Money comes back faster
  • Cashflows shift earlier
  • Time-value is higher
  • Annualized return increases

Example:

  • 20-year home loan at 9%: IRR ≈ 9%
  • Same loan prepaid in 5 years: IRR ≈ 9.3–9.5%

4.2.2 Why Banks Still Hate Prepayment

Because banks optimize for total interest income (NII), not IRR.

Scenario IRR Total Interest Earned
20-year home loan held to maturity ~9% ~₹1.4 crore
Same loan prepaid after 5 years ~9.3% ~₹40 lakh

The bank earns 3.5x less money on the prepaid loan even though the IRR is higher.

Analogy: Imagine investing in a shop that pays ₹1 lakh/month for 20 years vs ₹30 lakh after 6 months. The second has higher IRR, but the first earns far more total money.

4.2.3 Why Banks Charge Prepayment Penalties

Banks actively discourage early repayment:

  • Prepayment penalties (1–5% of outstanding)
  • Lock-in periods (no prepayment for first 1–3 years)
  • Foreclosure charges (if you close the entire loan early)
  • Partial prepayment limits (max 25% per year)

All designed to protect their interest income stream.

TipThe Key Distinction

IRR is a rate of return (efficiency). NII is absolute revenue (total money earned). Banks are businesses that need revenue, not just efficiency.

4.3 Duration, Tenure, and ROTA

4.3.1 Shorter Tenure → Higher IRR

In short-tenure loans:

  • Capital returns quickly
  • Fees/subvention are upfront
  • Principal reduces fast
  • Cashflows are early (high time value)
Tenure Capital Turnover IRR Character
3 months 4× per year Very high
6 months 2× per year High
12 months 1× per year Moderate
60 months 0.2× per year Low

4.3.2 ROTA = IRR × Capital Turnover (Conceptually)

ROTA (Return on Total Assets) captures both:

  • How much you earn per loan (yield)
  • How many times you can reuse capital (turnover)

\[\text{ROTA} \approx \text{Net Margin per Loan} \times \text{Capital Turnover}\]

This is why checkout finance NBFCs (Bajaj, TVS Credit) can earn 4–6% ROTA despite thin margins per loan — they turn capital 2–4 times per year.

4.3.3 The Missing Piece: Gross Yield ≠ Net Yield

Short tenure has higher IRR, but also:

  • Higher per-loan origination cost (relative to loan size)
  • Higher collection frequency cost
  • Higher default rate (in some segments)
  • Potentially lower subvention per transaction

So ROTA improves with short tenure only if the lender can keep costs proportionally low. This is why operational efficiency is the moat for consumer finance NBFCs.

4.4 Capital Turns: The Secret Metric

The most powerful concept in consumer lending:

\[\text{Annual Return} = \text{Per-Loan Profit} \times \text{Turns per Year}\]

Example:

Metric 6-month EMI 24-month PL
Subvention 8% —
Interest yield — 18%
Net margin per loan 3% 8%
Capital turns/year 2× 0.5×
Annual return on capital 6% 4%

Even though the PL earns more per loan, the EMI product wins on annual return because capital is recycled faster.

NoteMemorize This

IRR goes up when money returns fast. But lenders earn less total money when loans close early. Banks hate prepayment because revenue (absolute interest) is their goal, not mathematical IRR.
