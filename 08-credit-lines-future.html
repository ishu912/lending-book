8  Credit Lines, UPI & The Future of Lending

8.1 The Big Idea: PL as a Credit Line at Checkout

What if all personal loans came with a credit line that could be used instantly at merchant checkout?

This means:

  • Bank gives you a pre-approved credit limit (say ₹2 lakh)
  • You don’t take the loan immediately
  • You use it instantly at checkout via UPI
  • EMI is created on-the-spot
  • Money flows directly to merchant
  • You pay EMIs on the used amount only
  • You can use the line multiple times (revolving)

This is essentially a fully regulated personal loan + overdraft hybrid on UPI rails. It’s RBI’s dream design.

8.2 What Would Change: Impact by Player

8.2.1 For Customers: The Best Credit Product in India

  • Instant approval at checkout
  • Much cheaper interest rate than BNPL or card EMI
  • Works everywhere (UPI QR, online, offline)
  • No MDR cost passed to merchants
  • Simple: one line, multiple small loans
  • Transparent EMI conversion
  • Lower over-indebtedness risk than BNPL

8.2.2 For Banks: A Goldmine

  • Direct access to merchant checkout (no POS dependency)
  • High capital turnover from short EMIs
  • PL-quality underwriting (salaried, pre-approved)
  • Very low acquisition cost via UPI rails
  • Combines cheap CASA capital + moderate interest + high IRR
  • Becomes the most profitable retail loan category

8.2.3 For Merchants: Best Possible Scenario

  • Zero MDR (UPI-based)
  • Zero subvention (customer bears interest, or rate is low enough)
  • Instant checkout conversion
  • More customers can afford larger baskets
  • No disputes, no chargebacks
  • UPI QR acceptance stays the same

8.2.4 For NBFCs: Massive Disruption

NBFC checkout finance (Bajaj, TVS Credit) lives on merchant subvention, short-tenure EMI, POS presence, and OEM tie-ups. If banks offer PL credit lines at checkout:

  • No subvention needed
  • No POS counter needed
  • Faster and cheaper
  • Higher approval rates (salary-based)
  • Lower loss rates

NBFCs lose their core advantage. Their POS distribution moat collapses.

8.2.5 For BNPL: Nearly Dead

BNPL exists because PL is slow, can’t be used at checkout, and has high onboarding friction. If PL becomes instant credit line:

  • Cheaper, available everywhere, safer, regulated, no merchant cost
  • BNPL becomes unnecessary except for thin-file, low-income users

8.2.6 For Credit Cards: Significant Cannibalization

Credit cards survive because they offer instant credit, 45-day free period, and EMI at checkout. If PL credit lines match these features:

  • Cheaper interest than cards
  • No MDR for merchants
  • Better for high-ticket purchases
  • Cards retain advantage in rewards, international use, and premium experience

8.3 UPI Credit Line: The RBI Framework

RBI has been enabling this through policy:

  1. UPI on credit — allowing credit instruments on UPI rails
  2. Pre-approved credit lines — banks can offer instant lines
  3. Digital KYC — reducing onboarding friction
  4. Account Aggregator — enabling real-time underwriting from bank data

The infrastructure is converging to make PL-at-checkout a reality.

8.4 What Prevents Full Disruption (For Now)

Despite the potential, several barriers slow adoption:

  1. Bank technology lag: Most banks can’t do real-time, per-transaction credit decisions at scale
  2. Risk model immaturity: Banks don’t have checkout-level risk models (what product, what merchant, what time)
  3. Operational readiness: Banks aren’t set up for high-volume, small-ticket lending operations
  4. NBFC lobbying: Established players resist disruption
  5. Customer awareness: Most customers don’t know this option exists
  6. Merchant integration: UPI credit line requires merchant-side acceptance flow changes

8.5 The Convergence Thesis

The future of Indian lending likely converges toward:

  • UPI as the universal payment rail (including credit)
  • Banks providing capital (cheap CASA funding)
  • Fintechs providing technology (risk models, UX, merchant integration)
  • NBFCs finding niches (thin-file customers, specific merchant verticals)
  • Credit cards becoming premium products (rewards, international, lifestyle)

The checkout becomes the new branch. The credit line becomes the new loan application. And the line between payments and lending disappears entirely.

NoteMemorize This

PL credit lines at UPI checkout would be zero cost for merchants, cheaper for customers, and a goldmine for banks. It disrupts NBFCs, kills BNPL, and cannibalizes cards. The infrastructure exists — execution is the bottleneck.
